What This Document Is

This document contains evidence-based documentation items for three primary rehabilitation documentation frameworks used across physical therapy (PT), occupational therapy (OT), and speech-language pathology (SLP):

  1. Initial Evaluation — Comprehensive baseline assessment establishing the plan of care, functional limitations, goals, and medical necessity for rehabilitation services
  2. Progress Note (Daily Treatment Note) — Visit-by-visit documentation of skilled interventions, patient response, objective measurements, and ongoing medical necessity
  3. Re-evaluation / Discharge Summary — Comprehensive comparison of current status to initial evaluation, goal achievement, revised plan of care (if continuing), or discharge recommendations

Each framework captures rehabilitation encounters but organizes information differently based on encounter type, clinical setting, documentation purpose, and regulatory requirements. An Initial Evaluation establishes the complete clinical and functional picture. A Progress Note documents each treatment session's skilled interventions and patient response. A Re-evaluation/Discharge Summary compares outcomes to baseline and justifies continued care or documents discharge.

The key design principle: OmniScribe's AI engine captures everything during the rehabilitation encounter — the full conversation, examination findings, clinical reasoning, skilled interventions, patient response, and functional outcomes — then formats the captured data into the appropriate framework and subtype selected by the provider. The provider does not need to think about structure during the encounter. They assess, treat, and make clinical decisions. OmniScribe organizes.

How the Three Frameworks Relate

The three frameworks are not competing alternatives — they serve different documentation purposes within the same patient's rehabilitation episode:

  • A patient begins physical therapy with an Initial Evaluation (PT subtype) that establishes the plan of care. Each subsequent visit generates a Progress Note documenting skilled interventions and patient response. At the certification period renewal (every 90 days for Medicare), a Re-evaluation compares current status to baseline. When goals are met, a Discharge Summary documents outcomes.
  • A patient receiving both PT and OT services gets separate Initial Evaluations from each discipline (PT and OT subtypes). Each discipline generates its own Progress Notes and Re-evaluations. OmniScribe can capture a concurrent PT/OT session and output both notes simultaneously.
  • An inpatient receives PT, OT, and SLP services. Each discipline performs its own Initial Evaluation, generates daily Progress Notes, and completes discipline-specific Discharge Summaries. CMS Section GG data is shared across disciplines and settings.

OmniScribe's engine captures the encounter once and can output documentation in any of the three frameworks simultaneously when multiple documentation types are generated from a single encounter.

How Subtypes Work

Each of the three frameworks contains 5 subtypes representing different provider types within that framework's scope.

The selection flow:

  1. Framework Selection — The provider (or OmniScribe's AI based on encounter context) selects one of the three frameworks: Initial Evaluation, Progress Note, or Re-evaluation/Discharge.
  2. Subtype Selection — Within the selected framework, the provider selects one of 5 subtypes based on their discipline and credential: PT, PTA (→PT alias), OT, OTA (→OT alias), or SLP.
  3. Default Template Loads — The selected framework + subtype combination loads a default set of documentation items with format templates and source citations.
  4. Provider Customization — Providers can add or remove items from the default template to match the specific encounter's needs. Not every item is required for every encounter — the template represents the comprehensive set from which the provider selects.
  5. Specialty Add-Ons — Specialty-specific item sets layer additional documentation items on top of the base template. A hand therapist using the OT Initial Evaluation would see general OT items plus Hand Therapy add-on items. A PT treating vestibular dysfunction would see general PT items plus Vestibular Rehab add-on items.
  6. Cross-Setting Compatibility — CMS Section GG items are included in all frameworks and subtypes to ensure consistency across post-acute care settings (SNF, IRF, HHA, LTCH, outpatient).

PART 1: INITIAL EVALUATION FRAMEWORK

The Initial Evaluation is the foundation of every rehabilitation episode. It establishes the patient's baseline functional status, identifies impairments and activity limitations, sets measurable goals, creates the plan of care, and documents the medical necessity that justifies skilled rehabilitation services. CMS requires that initial evaluations be performed by the licensed therapist (PT, OT, or SLP) — assistants (PTA, OTA) may collect data elements under the therapist's direction but cannot independently perform the evaluation, establish the plan of care, or determine medical necessity.

All format templates use __ (double underscore) to indicate AI-populated fields. Pipes (|) within templates separate discrete data elements. Sources reference real clinical guidelines, coding standards, validated instruments, and regulatory frameworks.

PART 2: PROGRESS NOTE FRAMEWORK (DAILY TREATMENT NOTES)

The Progress Note documents each treatment session, capturing skilled interventions with CPT codes, treatment time for 8-Minute Rule compliance, patient response, objective re-measurements, clinical reasoning, goal progress, and ongoing medical necessity. Every treatment visit must demonstrate that the services provided require the skill of a licensed therapist (or assistant under therapist direction) and that the patient continues to benefit from or require skilled rehabilitation services.

Progress Notes follow a modified SOAP structure adapted for rehabilitation: Subjective (patient report, functional update, HEP compliance), Objective (skilled interventions, re-assessments, outcome measures), Assessment (clinical reasoning, goal status, prognosis), and Plan (next session, HEP, communication, discharge planning).

PART 3: RE-EVALUATION / DISCHARGE SUMMARY FRAMEWORK

The Re-evaluation / Discharge Summary provides a comprehensive comparison of current functional status to initial evaluation baseline, documents goal achievement, updates prognosis, and either revises the plan of care for continued services or documents discharge. CMS requires re-evaluation at certification period renewal (typically every 90 days), when there is a significant change in the patient's condition, or when the plan of care needs modification. Discharge summaries document the episode of care outcomes and provide recommendations for ongoing care.

Re-evaluations must be performed by the licensed therapist (PT, OT, or SLP) — assistants may contribute data collection but cannot independently determine whether to continue or discharge services. Discharge summaries by assistants require co-signature by the supervising therapist.

PART 4: REHABILITATION SPECIALTY ADD-ONS

Specialty add-ons are supplemental item sets that layer on top of the base framework templates. When a provider's practice area or a specific patient's condition requires specialized documentation beyond the base template, the appropriate add-on is activated. Add-ons are discipline-aware — some apply primarily to PT (e.g., Sports Rehab), some to OT (e.g., Hand Therapy), some to SLP (e.g., Voice/Swallowing is already in the base SLP template), and some cross disciplines (e.g., Wound Care, Aquatic Therapy).

Each add-on provides 10-15 additional items with the same format: Item Label | "format template" | Source. These items supplement — they do not replace — the base framework items.
